WestMEC Law, Public Safety & Security

What is our program?

WestMEC's two-year Law, Public Safety & Security program explores the basic skills used in patrol functions, defensive tactics, use of force and first aid. Students will learn constitutional law, forensic science, first aid / CPR, and more.


Upon completion of the two-year program, students may test to receive Certified Protection Officer Certification, Arizona Security License, First Aid, CPR and AED Management of Aggressive Behaviors certifications.

Our Curriculum

We build our curriculum off the experience and advice of industry professionals.

Our curriculum comes from an advisory council of industry professionals who meet and discuss skills required to obtain a career in the field. Each year the curriculum is updated to match the current industry standards. This helps us better prepare students to get work in the field.


Students are obtaining real workplace skills that can be used in a career in law enforcement. West-MEC's program provides the opportunity for students to take part in hands on experiences. At the end of the program students will have the opportunity to take industry certification exams.

Graduate with Industry Certification

Prepare for your future.

West-MEC's Law, Public Safety & Security program gives students hands on experience but it also provides students with the opportunity to take industry certification exams.


These exams cover all different topics relating to Law Enforcement from CPR Certification to Arizona Department of Public Safety Security Guard Certification. These certifications give students an edge when applying to positions in their field.
